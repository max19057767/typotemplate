Who I am

My name is Max Kielesinska, I am a second year student at OBU studying Media, Journalism & Publishing. Before coming to university I studied journalism in college, I wanted to pursue further education in a field where I could continue to develop my creative writing skills.


I am living in Oxford for the duration of my university course, but in my free time I often travel. One of my favourite places which I have visited in the past was Sicily, I stayed in Catania where I tasted Sicilian cuisine for the first time. During my stay I also visited Syracuse, which is known for its rich Roman and Greek history, and a beautiful town called Taormina.


In the future, I wish to direct my career path towards an artistic medium meanwhile implementing the tools and knowledge I acquired in the course of my studies. I would like to return to doing art as a profession or education, rather than solely creating pieces in my free time.



Where I am from

I grew up in Karpacz, Poland and at the age of 10 I moved to England.


The two photographs below this text are of Śnieżka, a mountain positioned on the border between Czech Republic and Poland, it is also the view from my bedroom window at my home in Karpacz.


If you are interested in reading more about Karpacz, its geographical placement and history, I recommend reading StayPoland's article about the Lower Silesian town.


What I like

From a young age I attended creative classes, as the years passed my passion and interest in art grew. I studied art and design along with photography for two years at Sixth Form, my education shaped my future goals and aspirations and as a result of that I aim to work as an archiver. In time outside of university classes I enjoy researching and curating my favourite artists and pieces, I am working towards creating a series of zines to display my own artwork and photographs.


My university course has allowed me to explore a variety of skills, expanding my knowledge and creative capabilities. One of the skills I have picked up over the course of my studies links to my recent interest in typography, as part of my course I have started writing blog entries about the different typefaces seen on book covers, commenting on their appearance, use and effect.
